# Example Claims Matrix

## Claim 1

Claim: Evidence-first PPT work should separate research, source audit, and slide generation.

Status: accepted

Evidence: S2, S3, S4

Reasoning: NIST supports documented AI risk and governance practices; GPT Researcher and ppt-master documentation support the research and slide-generation roles. The workflow adds an audit gate between them.

Deck wording: "Use a gated workflow: research first, audit every claim, then generate slides from approved material."

Risk/caveat: This supports the workflow design, not a claim that the workflow guarantees factual correctness.

## Claim 2

Claim: Market and adoption context should be treated as directional unless backed by current primary or high-quality research.

Status: needs caveat

Evidence: S1, S2

Reasoning: Stanford AI Index provides broad AI context, while NIST supports risk-aware treatment of AI claims. Topic-specific adoption claims still need stronger, current evidence before entering a final deck.

Deck wording: "Use broad AI adoption signals as context, then verify topic-specific claims before they enter the deck."

Risk/caveat: Do not convert broad AI trends into SMB-specific statistics without a dedicated source.
